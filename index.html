About Me

I am a biostatistician with 8 years of experience in the design and analysis of clinical trials and real-world evidence (RWE) studies. My expertise lies in applying advanced statistical methodologies to generate meaningful insights in oncology, cardiology, infectious diseases, and healthcare outcomes research. I specialize in survival analysis, mixed-effects modeling, causal inference, and machine learning, with strong proficiency in SAS, R, and SQL.

I hold an MS in Biostatistics from Drexel University, a BS from Tribhuvan University, and a graduate certificate in Bioinformatics from the University of Delaware. Recently, I have been particularly interested in causal inference methods and their applications in healthcare economics and outcomes research, aiming to improve decision-making and patient care through rigorous data analysis.

My research has been published in high-impact journals, and I actively contribute to the scientific community as a member of the American Statistical Association (ASA), the International Biometric Society (ENAR), and the Association for Clinical and Translational Science (ACTS).

On the personal side, I am passionate about community service, volleyball, and hiking. I play for the Everest Volleyball Team, which was the defending champion of the City of Newark Winter Volleyball League (B Division). Beyond sports, I have a deep love for the outdoors and have hiked many peaks across the United States and Nepal, embracing the challenge and adventure that come with exploring nature.

Feel free to connect with me on LinkedIn or explore my research on Google Scholar.
